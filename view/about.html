HISTORIC ROTU ARMY UMP

Published on : 15 AUG 2021

The early history of PALAPES began with the establishment of the First Battalion of the Territorial Army Infantry, University of Malaya Branch on 3 April 1965. At the beginning of its establishment, 30 students from various faculties underwent Basic Recruitment courses at Seputih Training Camp, Batu Gajah, Perak, during semester breaks.

The May 13 event saw this company expanded to other public universities with the establishment of Company A in ITM, Company B in UPM and Company C in UKM with UM being the headquarters of its Battalion. Following the encouraging response from the students, the Director of the Territorial Army, Brigadier General Dato 'Abdul As Ismail, took the initiative to discuss with the vice-chancellors of the University and the director of ITM at that time to establish a Reserve Officer Training Unit (ROTU).

On 18 December 1978, the Armed Forces Staff Committee approved the establishment of ROTU with the main objective of producing volunteer military officers from among lecturers, staff and students in public universities. On 26 February 1979, a meeting chaired by the Chief of Staff of the Army and attended by the Vice Chancellors of the University and the Director of ITM was held to establish ROTU in their respective IPTAs.
